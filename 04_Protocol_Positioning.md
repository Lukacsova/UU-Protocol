# UU Protocol: Proof of Human Origin

## Overview

The UU Protocol introduces a new primitive for the internet: **proof of human origin**.

It enables digital systems to recognize **human-origin participation** without identity systems, personal data collection, or storage.

---

## The Missing Capability

Digital systems cannot reliably distinguish human-origin activity from automated or synthetic participation. This limitation creates structural challenges for digital platforms as AI agents, automated systems, and synthetic identities proliferate.

---

## Proof of Human Origin

The UU Protocol generates **Human-Origin Proofs (HOPs)** — cryptographic events derived from physical human action bound to space and time.

Each proof produces a **Human-Origin Attestation**, recognized as a **human-origin signal**.  
No identity data is collected, tracked, or stored.

---

## Privacy-Preserving Architecture

- Zero-storage architecture
- No identity databases
- No behavioral tracking
- No persistent records

Human-Origin Proofs are generated and recognized while preserving user privacy.

---

## Neutral and Permissionless Participation

- The protocol is **neutral infrastructure**.
- **Permissionless** HOP generation for users.
- Accessible globally, independent of centralized control.

---

## Protecting Protocol Integrity

HOPs, POH, and POI are cryptographically uncorruptible.  
Alterations or deployment outside intended use could enable:

- behavioral tracking
- identity surveillance
- large-scale data extraction
- exploitation of human participation

Stewardship and intellectual property protect the protocol integrity.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The UU Protocol aims to provide **neutral, privacy-preserving infrastructure** to recognize human participation online.
